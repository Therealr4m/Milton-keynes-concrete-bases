Why shed foundations are important

Many sheds begin to suffer from movement or sicking doors if they aren't sat on a stable foundation. A properly installed shed foundation provides a permanent level platform that distributes the shed's weight evenly while reducing future movement caused by settlement or unstable ground.

Unlike temporary paving slabs or timber bearers, a reinforced concrete foundation offers long-term durability with very little maintenance. It creates a solid base capable of supporting everything from compact storage sheds through to larger workshops and heavy-duty garden buildings while helping protect the structure from moisture and uneven ground conditions.

How We Install Concrete Shed Foundations

Every shed foundation follows a structured installation process to ensure the finished slab provides a stable, long-lasting platform for the building it will support. By carrying out each stage correctly, we reduce the likelihood of movement, settlement and future structural issues.

Site Survey & Measuring

We begin by measuring the proposed shed location, checking access and assessing the existing ground conditions. This allows us to determine the correct foundation dimensions and installation requirements before excavation begins.

Excavation

The installation area is excavated to the appropriate depth, removing soft material and creating space for a properly constructed sub-base beneath the finished concrete foundation.

Sub-Base Preparation

A compacted MOT Type 1 sub-base is installed to create a firm platform capable of supporting the concrete slab. This stage plays a significant role in reducing future settlement.

Formwork & Reinforcement

Timber shuttering is installed to produce clean, accurate edges before reinforcement is positioned where required to increase structural strength across the foundation.

Concrete Pour & Finishing

The concrete is placed, levelled and professionally finished to create a smooth, consistent surface that is ready to support the shed once sufficient curing has taken place.

Final Inspection

Before completing the project we inspect the finished foundation, check levels and ensure the slab is ready for the shed installation.
