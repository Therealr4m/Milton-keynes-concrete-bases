Our Newport Pagnell Concrete Base Process

Every concrete base is installed using a structured process that focuses on long-term performance. Proper preparation beneath the slab is just as important as the concrete itself, helping to create a foundation that remains stable for years to come.

Initial Site Assessment

We begin by discussing your project, measuring the proposed installation area and checking site access. This allows us to understand the size of the required base while identifying anything that may influence the installation.

Excavation

The existing ground is excavated to the appropriate depth, removing soft or unsuitable material and creating sufficient space for the construction of a stable sub-base beneath the finished concrete.

Sub-Base Preparation

A compacted MOT Type 1 sub-base is installed to provide a strong, load-bearing platform. This stage helps distribute weight evenly while significantly reducing the likelihood of future settlement.

Timber Formwork

Accurate timber shuttering is installed around the perimeter to create clean edges and ensure the finished slab is built to the required dimensions.

Reinforcement

Where the project requires additional structural strength, reinforcement is incorporated into the slab before the concrete is poured. This provides increased durability for heavier garden buildings and higher load applications.

Concrete Placement

Fresh concrete is poured, levelled and professionally finished to produce a smooth, even surface suitable for the intended structure once curing has taken place.

Final Quality Checks

Before the project is completed, we inspect the finished concrete base, confirm the levels and ensure everything has been finished to a high professional standard.
