Our Great Linford Concrete Base Installation Process

We keep the installation process straightforward. The exact work depends on the size of the base, the proposed building and the condition of the existing ground, but every job is planned around the site rather than treated as a standard slab.

Initial Site Assessment

We discuss what you are planning to build, look at the proposed position and consider the available access. This gives us the information needed to work out the appropriate size and installation approach.

Excavation

The installation area is excavated to remove unsuitable surface material and create space for the prepared sub-base and concrete slab.

Sub-Base Preparation

A suitable MOT Type 1 sub-base is installed and compacted to create a firm platform beneath the concrete. The amount of preparation required depends on the existing ground and the demands of the finished structure.

Formwork And Reinforcement

Timber formwork is set out to the required dimensions. Where the project calls for it, reinforcement is positioned before the concrete is placed.

Concrete Placement And Finishing

Fresh concrete is poured, levelled and finished to provide the surface required for the intended building. The work is then checked before the site is handed back.

The Base Needs To Suit The Building Going On Top

A small garden shed and a larger workshop do not place the same demands on the foundation beneath them. The dimensions, position and intended use of the building all need to be considered before the base is installed. That is why we do not simply arrive and pour a standard slab. We look at the actual project, the available space and the ground conditions so the installation is planned around what you are building.
