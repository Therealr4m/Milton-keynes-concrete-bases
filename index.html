Frequent Concrete Base Installation Issues

Poor preparation and rushed installation are some of the biggest reasons concrete bases fail prematurely. Many homeowners contact us after discovering problems with an existing base that has become unsuitable for the structure it was built to support.

  • Uneven concrete bases causing buildings to sit out of level
  • Cracking concrete caused by poor ground preparation or inadequate reinforcement
  • Concrete bases sinking due to insufficient excavation and sub-base compaction
  • Standing water caused by poor drainage and incorrect finished levels
  • Concrete surfaces that become soft, dusty or begin to break down
  • Bases installed too small for the intended structure
  • Poor edge finishing leading to crumbling and deterioration over time
  • Movement caused by unstable ground conditions beneath the slab
  • Incorrect concrete thickness reducing long-term strength
  • Tree roots and ground movement affecting structural stability
  • Poorly compacted MOT Type 1 sub-base causing settlement
  • Concrete poured without reinforcement where reinforcement was required

Correct excavation, sub-base preparation and accurate levelling as well as reinforced concrete all play an important role in creating a realiable concrete base that'll last many years.

Our MK Concrete Base Installation Process

Every concrete base we install follows the same proven process, whether it's a small shed base or a large commercial workshop foundation. Good concrete starts long before the concrete arrives, so every stage is carried out properly to produce a base that's level, durable and built to last.

Site Assessment

We begin by assessing access, measuring the installation area and identifying any ground conditions that could affect the project. This allows us to determine the correct excavation depth, base size and installation method before work begins.

Excavation & Ground Preparation

The installation area is excavated to the required depth before a compacted sub-base is installed. Proper preparation is one of the most important stages, as it provides the stable foundation that supports the finished concrete base.

Formwork & Reinforcement

Timber formwork is installed to achieve accurate dimensions and clean edges. Where required, steel reinforcement is added to improve structural strength and long-term durability.

Concrete Installation

The concrete is poured, levelled and finished using professional techniques to achieve a smooth, even surface with consistent thickness throughout the entire base.

Curing & Final Inspection

Once installed, the concrete is allowed to cure correctly before the site is tidied and the completed base is inspected to ensure it is ready to support the intended structure.

Different Concrete Base Installation Methods

Not every concrete base is installed the same way. The correct construction method depends on what the base is supporting, the ground conditions, drainage requirements and the long-term structural loads. A small garden shed requires a very different approach compared to a garage, workshop or brick outbuilding.

Standard Reinforced Concrete Bases

Most residential concrete bases are installed using a properly excavated and compacted sub-base, reinforced concrete and accurate finishing. This provides a stable platform for sheds, summerhouses, garden offices, hot tubs and similar outdoor buildings.

Strip Foundations

Where a structure will be built using brick or blockwork, strip foundations can provide a cost-effective solution. Concrete is placed beneath the load-bearing walls while the remaining floor construction is completed separately, making this a practical option for many masonry buildings.

Trench Fill Foundations

For larger or more demanding structural projects, trench fill foundations can reduce labour and construction time by filling the foundation trench with concrete to near ground level. Although this method uses more concrete, it often speeds up the overall build and reduces the amount of blockwork required.

Foundations Built Around Your Project

Rather than using the same specification on every installation, we assess each project individually. Factors such as the intended structure, ground conditions, access, loading requirements and future use all influence the most suitable foundation or concrete base construction method.
